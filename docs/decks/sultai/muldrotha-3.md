## Wincons (clear + brutal)

* **Living Death** swing turn (often ends the game within a turn cycle).
* **Syr Konrad** turns self-mill/recursion into table drain (especially with Living Death).
* **Lord of Extinction + Jarad** converts “big yard game” into direct life loss.
* **Massacre Wurm** as the “board + life total” hammer on recursion turns.

## Graveyard-hate resilience (you asked for durability)

* **Kill the hate:** Nature’s Claim / Krosan Grip / Trophy / Beast Within + Boseiju/Otawara.
* **Don’t fold to exile:** **Perpetual Timepiece** (tuck key cards) + **The One Ring** (play fair midrange if yard gets hit).
* **Play pattern:** don’t overfill the yard unless you can **cash out now** (Living Death / Konrad turn).

# Tier ratings + final recommendation (brutal)

### **Package A (this authoritative list): Survival + One Ring + Force of Will**

**Tier: A / “Top Bracket 3”**
Most consistent **without** leaning on “Rift ends it” or turbo starts. Best *durability* and best *post-hate recovery*.

### **Package B (Control + Closer): Rhystic + Cyclonic Rift + Fierce**

**Tier: A- (or A if your pod is spell-heavy)**
Higher “I end it now” conversion, but it’s more **table-salt prone** and more likely to feel like “Bracket 4 wearing a hat,” even if legal. Also less “toolbox inevitability” than Survival.

### **Package C (Turbo): Ancient Tomb + Mana Vault + Chrome Mox**

**Tier: A in win-rate, C in “Bracket 3 vibe”**
If your pod says “tuned Bracket 3” but actually plays like Bracket 4, this wins more. If your pod is *actually* Bracket 3-minded, this gets you targeted and called out.

## Final recommendation

Run **Package A** as your default “authoritative” build. It’s the strongest version that’s still genuinely Bracket 3 in *how it plays*: inevitable, interactive, and durable—without screaming “I’m cheating the bracket.”
