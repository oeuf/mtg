## Wincons (what actually ends the game)

This deck wins by **inevitability → cash-out**, not by a compact 2-card insta-win.

1. **Living Death turn** *(primary)*
   You self-mill + trade resources, then Living Death rebuilds you and nukes opponents’ boards—often lethal within a turn cycle.

2. **Syr Konrad drain** *(primary)*
   Konrad turns self-mill + recursion turns into reliable table drain; it’s especially brutal alongside Living Death and repeated creature churn.

3. **Lord of Extinction + Jarad** *(primary)*
   Lord gets enormous in a 4-player game; Jarad converts that into life loss (often “end two players now, kill last next”).

4. **Massacre Wurm** *(secondary but real)*
   A single recursion swing can erase token boards and chunk life totals enough that Konrad/Jarad finishes.

Brutal critique: you don’t have a “clean, deterministic combo kill” by design—**this is how you stay Bracket 3 in spirit**.

## Graveyard hate plan (durable, not cope)

Your main weakness is obvious: **graveyard hate turns off your commander text**. ([Gatherer][2])
So the deck is built to *not instantly fold*:

### 1) Preempt / punish

* **Dauthi Voidwalker**: turns opposing graveyard plans off and steals their best card.
* **Bojuka Bog + Soul-Guide Lantern**: flexible hate you can recur with Muldrotha when needed.

### 2) Protect your key cards from “exile yard”

* **Perpetual Timepiece**: lets you tuck key pieces back (so a blowout exile doesn’t end you).

### 3) Kill the hate immediately

* **Nature’s Claim / Krosan Grip / Beast Within / Trophy / Boseiju / Otawara**: enough density that “Rest in Peace”-style pieces usually die on sight.

### 4) Sequencing discipline (this matters more than people admit)

Don’t dump half your deck into the yard unless you can **cash out now** (Living Death, Konrad turn, or immediate board build). Otherwise you invite the one-shot exile.
