# Package 1: Teval Lands Engine (most “Teval-specific” inevitability)

### **GCs (exactly 3)**

* **Field of the Dead (GC)** ([MAGIC: THE GATHERING][1])
* **Glacial Chasm (GC)** ([MAGIC: THE GATHERING][1])
* **Crop Rotation (GC)** ([MAGIC: THE GATHERING][1])

**What this changes:** you become **inevitable via lands**, and you get a “panic button” (Crop Rotation → Chasm) that the thread calls out as a big reason Teval can keep up in high-power pods. ([Reddit][3])

### Non-GC swaps to support it (from your pool)

(Assuming your current baseline is the tuned list with **Rhystic/Rift/Fierce**)

**Cut these (because you’re moving away from blue “close/control” and into land inevitability):**

* **Rhystic Study (GC)** → becomes Field (GC)
* **Cyclonic Rift (GC)** → becomes Glacial Chasm (GC)
* **Fierce Guardianship (GC)** → becomes Crop Rotation (GC)

Then tighten the 99 to maximize “lands engine does the work”:

**Add (non-GC)**

* **Zuran Orb** (lets you cash lands; supports replay lines and survival)
* **Icetill Explorer** (thread explicitly mentions it as a way to keep Chasm online) ([Reddit][3])
* **Perpetual Timepiece** (anti-exile / reshuffle protection for key lands)
* **Strip Mine** (keep problem lands in check while you’re protected)
* **Wasteland** (same job; redundancy)

**Cut (non-GC)**

* **Mana Drain** (you’ll tap out more; Drain is best in “hold up” shells)
* **Force of Negation** (same logic—your protection becomes Chasm-based)
* **Counterspell / Arcane Denial** (trim 1–2; you can’t be pure permission *and* lands engine)
* **Howling Mine / Jayemdae Tome / Library of Lat-Nam** (slow/low impact vs tuned pods if you had any of these in your 99)

**Result:** You’re “Teval Lands” — you win by **making land drops matter** and using **Chasm + recursion** to ensure you get to the endgame.

> **Warning (social, not rules):** The thread also calls out that repeated Chasm recursion is “groan-inducing / archenemy” even when technically Bracket 3. ([Reddit][3])

# Package 2: Tuned Pod Control + Closer (highest conversion rate)

### **GCs (exactly 3)**

* **Rhystic Study (GC)** ([MAGIC: THE GATHERING][1])
* **Cyclonic Rift (GC)** ([MAGIC: THE GATHERING][1])
* **Fierce Guardianship (GC)** ([MAGIC: THE GATHERING][1])

**What this changes:** you maximize **card volume**, **stack safety**, and **game-ending conversion** (Rift → win). This is the “best generic tuned Bracket 3” Teval package.

### Non-GC swaps to support it (from your pool)

**Add (non-GC)**

* **Mystic Remora** (extra early draw engine alongside Rhystic)
* **Sensei’s Divining Top** (topdeck smoothing for a deck that can churn)
* **Fact or Fiction** (instant-speed refill that plays well with holding up countermagic)
* **Soul-Guide Lantern** (hate + cantrip; helps vs mirror-y grave lines)
* **Pernicious Deed** (reset button that isn’t a GC and helps you stabilize to Rift)

**Cut (non-GC)**

* **Krosan Grip** → if your meta isn’t heavy on must-kill artifacts/enchantments (replace with cheaper interaction)
* **Putrefy** (usually worse than Trophy/Beast/Claim suite)
* **Some of the slower “lands-only” pieces** that are best when you’re on Chasm/Field (e.g., extra “cute” land recursion tech if you’re short on slots)

**Result:** You’re “Teval Control-Midrange” — Teval is still an engine, but **Rhystic + counters + Rift** is your consistent line to winning.
