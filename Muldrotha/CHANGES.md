# Muldrotha Deck Changes - Final Recommendations

**Analysis Date**: 2025-12-31
**Power Level Target**: Bracket 4 (7.5-8/10)
**Strategy**: 70% Midrange Value / 20% Altar Combos / 10% Thoracle Escape Hatch

---

## Executive Summary

This document details **15 cuts and 15 additions** to optimize your Muldrotha deck for competitive casual play while maintaining the 70/20/10 strategic balance. The changes address critical gaps in creature ramp, recurrable removal, and graveyard hate while removing underperforming cards and those that pull too heavily toward combo.

**Net Impact**: Improved consistency, better Muldrotha synergy, stronger midrange game plan.

---

## Cards to Cut (15)

### HIGH PRIORITY CUTS (7) - Cards Working Against Strategy

#### 1. Hermit Druid
**Category**: Self-Mill Creature
**Reason for Cut**: Too combo-focused for 70/20/10 strategy
**Analysis**: With only 11 basic lands, Hermit Druid threatens to mill your entire library in one activation. This creates an uncomfortable tension where you're either using it as an all-in combo piece or inefficiently as self-mill. The card signals "combo deck" to opponents, drawing removal and making you archenemy when you want to play midrange value. Nyx Weaver provides controlled self-mill without the combo implications.
**EDHREC Data**: 40% inclusion but controversial in non-combo builds
**Replacement**: Nyx Weaver

#### 2. Cultivate
**Category**: Ramp Sorcery
**Reason for Cut**: Too slow at 3 mana for Bracket 4
**Analysis**: At competitive casual tables, 3-mana ramp is inefficient. Nature's Lore at 2 mana finds shock lands untapped, providing both fixing and tempo advantage. Cultivate puts one land into hand (card disadvantage compared to alternatives) and is a sorcery (no Muldrotha recursion synergy).
**EDHREC Data**: 35% inclusion in average lists, but <20% in optimized builds
**Replacement**: Nature's Lore

#### 3. Six
**Category**: Creature (Lands-Matter)
**Reason for Cut**: Insufficient lands-matter support
**Analysis**: Six requires a dedicated lands strategy to shine. With only Exploration, Gitrog Monster, and Life from the Loam as land synergies, Six often becomes a 3-mana creature that plays one land from graveyard. Ramunap Excavator already fills this role more efficiently. Without fetch-sacrifice loops or heavy landfall triggers, Six underperforms.
**EDHREC Data**: 27% inclusion (lower than alternatives)
**Replacement**: Sakura-Tribe Elder provides better ramp synergy

#### 4. Tatyova, Benthic Druid
**Category**: Creature (Card Draw)
**Reason for Cut**: Not enough land drops to maximize value
**Analysis**: Tatyova shines when playing 2-3 lands per turn. With only Exploration and Gitrog Monster enabling extra land drops, Tatyova typically draws one card per turn cycle - fine but not exceptional at Bracket 4. Gitrog Monster already provides land-based card advantage more efficiently. The slot is better used for cards that synergize directly with Muldrotha's casting-from-graveyard ability.
**EDHREC Data**: 25% inclusion, 18% synergy (lower synergy than alternatives)
**Replacement**: River Kelpie draws whenever you cast from graveyard

#### 5. Dryad of the Ilysian Grove
**Category**: Creature (Color Fixing)
**Reason for Cut**: Unnecessary color fixing with this mana base
**Analysis**: Your mana base with 6 fetch lands, 3 shock lands, Command Tower, Arcane Signet, and Bloom Tender provides excellent color fixing. Dryad's primary benefit (making all lands all colors) is redundant. The extra land drop is nice but not essential without dedicated landfall triggers. The slot better serves the strategy with higher-synergy permanents.
**EDHREC Data**: 22% inclusion in optimized lists (cut for efficiency)
**Replacement**: Secrets of the Dead provides card draw engine

#### 6. Gravebreaker Lamia
**Category**: Creature (Tutor)
**Reason for Cut**: Redundant with better tutors
**Analysis**: With the addition of Demonic Tutor and Vampiric Tutor, Gravebreaker Lamia becomes redundant. At 5 mana, it's expensive for a tutor effect. While the lifelink body and graveyard placement is relevant, the cost-reduction ability doesn't impact enough cards in your deck to justify the slot. Better tutors are more efficient.
**EDHREC Data**: 47% inclusion but often first tutor cut in optimized lists
**Replacement**: Demonic Tutor (2 mana, any card)

#### 7. Demonic Consultation
**Category**: Instant (Combo Piece)
**Reason for Cut**: Too narrow, only works with Thoracle
**Analysis**: Demonic Consultation is exclusively a Thoracle combo piece. It represents 10% of your strategy (the escape hatch) but dedicating a card slot to a piece that's dead without Thassa's Oracle works against your 70% midrange goal. The card signals combo intent and draws hate. More importantly, the slot is better used for cards that support your primary midrange strategy while still maintaining Thoracle as an emergency win through natural draw.
**EDHREC Data**: Common in cEDH, less common in competitive casual
**Replacement**: Vampiric Tutor (finds Thoracle OR any other card you need)

### MEDIUM PRIORITY CUTS (8) - Good Cards, Better Alternatives

#### 8. Crop Rotation
**Category**: Instant (Utility Land Tutor)
**Reason for Cut**: Utility land tutor not essential
**Analysis**: Crop Rotation finds utility lands at instant speed, which is useful but not essential. Your utility lands (Strip Mine, Boseiju, Bojuka Bog, Takenuma) are good but not critical enough to warrant a dedicated tutor. With Life from the Loam recursion and strong card draw, you'll naturally find utility lands. The slot better serves card advantage or removal.
**EDHREC Data**: 18% inclusion in optimized lists (often cut)
**Replacement**: Birthing Pod (creature tutor with graveyard synergy)

#### 9. Veil of Summer
**Category**: Instant (Protection)
**Reason for Cut**: Meta-dependent, narrow
**Analysis**: Veil of Summer is excellent against blue/black control but dead against many archetypes (artifact combo, creature aggro, Gruul decks). You already have 6 counterspells plus Swan Song for interaction. The card is meta-dependent - powerful in some pods, useless in others. More universal protection or card advantage better serves the deck.
**EDHREC Data**: 15% inclusion (meta-call)
**Replacement**: Force of Will (universal protection)

#### 10. Pitiless Plunderer
**Category**: Creature (Combo Piece)
**Reason for Cut**: Altar piece but not essential
**Analysis**: Pitiless Plunderer creates treasure loops with sacrifice outlets, but your altar package (Gravecrawler + Phyrexian Altar, Bloodghast loops) doesn't require it. The card is good value when creatures die but not essential for your 20% altar strategy. With Syr Konrad already providing payoff, Pitiless Plunderer is redundant luxury.
**EDHREC Data**: 28% inclusion but often cut when optimizing
**Replacement**: Haywire Mite (recurrable removal)

#### 11. Diabolic Intent
**Category**: Sorcery (Tutor)
**Reason for Cut**: Weaker tutor being replaced
**Analysis**: Diabolic Intent requires sacrificing a creature, which has synergy with your graveyard strategy. However, at 1B with sacrifice cost, it's less efficient than Demonic Tutor (1B, no sacrifice) and Vampiric Tutor (B, instant speed). With three premium tutors (Demotic, Vampiric, Mystical, Worldly, Entomb, Buried Alive), Diabolic Intent is the weakest link.
**EDHREC Data**: 30% inclusion but cut when better tutors added
**Replacement**: Demonic Tutor (strictly better)

#### 12. Mana Vault
**Category**: Artifact (Fast Mana)
**Reason for Cut**: Fast mana but slot better utilized
**Analysis**: Mana Vault is good fast mana that helps deploy Muldrotha early. However, it damages you and requires mana to untap. With Sol Ring and Lotus Petal added as fast mana, plus improved creature ramp (Sakura-Tribe Elder, Nature's Lore), your ramp package is sufficient. The slot better serves card advantage or interaction at this power level.
**EDHREC Data**: 25% inclusion (luxury pick in non-cEDH)
**Replacement**: Lotus Petal (recurrable fast mana with higher Muldrotha synergy)

#### 13. Windfall
**Category**: Sorcery (Card Draw)
**Reason for Cut**: Good for graveyard filling but not essential
**Analysis**: Windfall fills your graveyard while disrupting opponent hands - a solid effect. However, it's a sorcery (no Muldrotha recursion) and can help opponents more than it hurts them in some situations. With the addition of Secrets of the Dead, Mulldrifter, and River Kelpie, your card draw package is strong enough without Windfall's variance.
**EDHREC Data**: 22% inclusion in optimized lists (often cut)
**Replacement**: Secrets of the Dead (consistent engine)

#### 14. Necromancy
**Category**: Enchantment (Reanimation)
**Reason for Cut**: Third reanimation spell is luxury
**Analysis**: Necromancy's flash ability is powerful, enabling instant-speed reanimation. However, with Animate Dead and Reanimate in the deck, a third reanimation spell is luxury rather than necessity. At 2B, it's more expensive than alternatives. The enchantment type synergizes with Muldrotha, but the slot better serves card advantage or tutors.
**EDHREC Data**: 32% inclusion (often 3rd reanimation cut)
**Replacement**: Seal of Primordium (recurrable removal)

#### 15. 1 Swamp
**Category**: Basic Land
**Reason for Cut**: Making room for Bojuka Bog
**Analysis**: Reducing basic count from 11 to 10 to accommodate Bojuka Bog. With excellent color fixing from fetch lands and shock lands, one fewer basic has minimal impact on mana consistency. Bojuka Bog's recurrable graveyard hate (via Life from the Loam) is worth the slight reduction in basic land count.
**EDHREC Data**: Average basic count in 3-color fetches is 10-12
**Replacement**: Bojuka Bog

## Cards to Add (15)

### TIER 1: ESSENTIAL ADDITIONS (8) - Critical Gaps

#### 1. Sol Ring
**Category**: Artifact (Fast Mana)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Medium (artifact permanent)
**Power Level**: 10/10
**Why Add**: The most glaring omission from your list. Sol Ring is essentially a free inclusion in every Commander deck. At Bracket 4, every opponent will have one, and starting turn 1 behind on mana puts unnecessary pressure on your game plan. Even without artifact recursion themes, Sol Ring accelerates Muldrotha deployment by a full turn.
**EDHREC Data**: 85% inclusion rate across all Muldrotha builds
**Strategic Impact**: Enables turn 3 Muldrotha consistently
**Replaces**: Should already be in deck

#### 2. Sakura-Tribe Elder
**Category**: Creature (Ramp)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: High (creature, death trigger, recurrable)
**Power Level**: 8/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: One of the highest-synergy ramp cards for Muldrotha. Provides ramp, death triggers for Syr Konrad, fills graveyard, and is infinitely recurrable through Muldrotha. At 2 mana with instant-speed activation, it's both efficient and flexible. Can be sacrificed in response to removal for value.
**EDHREC Data**: 50% inclusion, 26% synergy rating
**Strategic Impact**: Repeatable ramp engine with Muldrotha
**Replaces**: Six

#### 3. Demonic Tutor
**Category**: Sorcery (Tutor)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Low (sorcery, one-time use)
**Power Level**: 10/10
**Why Add**: The best tutor in Commander. At 1B, finds any card in your deck with no restrictions. Essential for consistency in Bracket 4 play. Finds combo pieces when needed, answers when required, or value engines when grinding. Despite being a sorcery, the raw power justifies inclusion.
**EDHREC Data**: 50% inclusion in optimized lists
**Strategic Impact**: Dramatically increases consistency
**Replaces**: Gravebreaker Lamia (more efficient tutor)

#### 4. Nature's Lore
**Category**: Sorcery (Ramp)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Low (sorcery)
**Power Level**: 8/10
**Why Add**: At 2 mana, finds shock lands (Breeding Pool, Watery Grave, Overgrown Tomb) untapped, providing both color fixing and tempo advantage over Cultivate. More efficient than 3-mana ramp at Bracket 4 tables where games are decided by turns 8-12. Guarantees land drop while maintaining card advantage.
**EDHREC Data**: Increasingly popular in optimized lists over Cultivate
**Strategic Impact**: Faster, more efficient ramp
**Replaces**: Cultivate

#### 5. Seal of Primordium
**Category**: Enchantment (Removal)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Very High (enchantment permanent, recurrable)
**Power Level**: 8/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: Fills critical gap in recurrable artifact/enchantment removal. At 2 mana to cast and 1 to activate, it's efficient removal that can be deployed proactively and held as insurance. With Muldrotha, becomes recurring answer to problematic permanents (Rhystic Study, Smothering Tithe, combo pieces). Being an enchantment triggers death synergies.
**EDHREC Data**: 46% inclusion, 41% synergy rating
**Strategic Impact**: Recurring removal engine
**Replaces**: Crop Rotation

#### 6. Haywire Mite
**Category**: Artifact Creature (Removal)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Very High (artifact creature, recurrable, death trigger)
**Power Level**: 8/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: One of the highest-synergy cards in Muldrotha specifically. Artifact creature type allows recursion as artifact OR creature through Muldrotha. 1-mana body provides early chump blocker or altar fodder. Death trigger destroys artifact or enchantment, making it recurrable removal. Perfect Muldrotha card.
**EDHREC Data**: 48% inclusion, 43% synergy rating (highest synergy artifact)
**Strategic Impact**: Flexible recurrable removal
**Replaces**: Pitiless Plunderer

#### 7. Bojuka Bog
**Category**: Land (Graveyard Hate)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: High (land, recurrable via Life from the Loam)
**Power Level**: 7/10
**Why Add**: Fills critical gap in graveyard hate. Current list is vulnerable to opposing graveyard strategies with no proactive answers. Bojuka Bog exiles opponent graveyards at minimal cost (enters tapped). With Life from the Loam and Crop Rotation (if kept), becomes recurrable graveyard hate that doesn't hurt your strategy.
**EDHREC Data**: 35% inclusion in optimized lists
**Strategic Impact**: Recurring graveyard hate
**Replaces**: 1 Swamp

#### 8. Lotus Petal
**Category**: Artifact (Fast Mana)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Extremely High (artifact permanent, recurrable)
**Power Level**: 9/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: The highest-synergy artifact in Muldrotha. Zero-mana artifact that can be replayed every turn through Muldrotha for free mana acceleration. Provides combo turn burst mana or consistent ramp in grind games. Fills graveyard, triggers death effects, enables early Muldrotha. Perfect permanent for this strategy.
**EDHREC Data**: 58% inclusion, 49% synergy (highest synergy rating)
**Strategic Impact**: Recurrable fast mana
**Replaces**: Mana Vault

### TIER 2: STRONG ADDITIONS (7) - High-Value Cards

#### 9. Mulldrifter
**Category**: Creature (Card Draw)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Very High (creature, evoke synergy, recurrable)
**Power Level**: 8/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: Evoke for 3 mana, draw 2 cards, put creature in graveyard. Recurrable through Muldrotha for repeatable card advantage. Can be hardcast as flying blocker when needed. Triggers death synergies with Syr Konrad. Simple, efficient, perfect for Muldrotha.
**EDHREC Data**: 29% inclusion, 20% synergy rating
**Strategic Impact**: Recurrable card advantage
**Replaces**: Windfall

#### 10. River Kelpie
**Category**: Creature (Card Draw)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Extremely High (triggers on casting from graveyard)
**Power Level**: 9/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: Draws a card whenever you cast a spell from your graveyard - directly synergizes with Muldrotha's ability. Also draws when persist creatures return. Creates explosive value loops where casting permanents from graveyard draws cards, which lets you cast more permanents. Pure gas in Muldrotha.
**EDHREC Data**: 20% inclusion, 13% synergy
**Strategic Impact**: Card advantage engine for core strategy
**Replaces**: Tatyova, Benthic Druid

#### 11. Secrets of the Dead
**Category**: Enchantment (Card Draw)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Extremely High (triggers on casting from graveyard)
**Power Level**: 9/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: Draws a card whenever you cast a spell from your graveyard. Enchantment permanent recurrable through Muldrotha. Costs only 2 mana. With Muldrotha active, casting 3 permanents from graveyard per turn means drawing 3 additional cards. Snowballs advantage rapidly. One of the best cards for this specific commander.
**EDHREC Data**: 43% inclusion, 40% synergy rating
**Strategic Impact**: Primary card advantage engine
**Replaces**: Dryad of the Ilysian Grove

#### 12. Nyx Weaver
**Category**: Enchantment Creature (Self-Mill)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Very High (enchantment creature, controlled self-mill, recursion)
**Power Level**: 8/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: Replaces Hermit Druid with controlled self-mill. Mills 2 cards during each of your upkeeps (predictable, safe). Enchantment creature type allows recursion as enchantment OR creature. Can sacrifice to return any card from graveyard to hand (value). Provides consistent graveyard filling without the all-in combo implications.
**EDHREC Data**: 32% inclusion, 28% synergy
**Strategic Impact**: Controlled graveyard engine
**Replaces**: Hermit Druid

#### 13. Vampiric Tutor
**Category**: Instant (Tutor)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Low (instant, one-time)
**Power Level**: 9/10
**Why Add**: Instant-speed tutor for only B mana. Use on opponent's end step to find exactly what you need for your turn. Finds combo pieces, answers, or value engines. Being instant-speed allows you to respond to board state and decide what you need after seeing opponent actions. Completes premium tutor suite.
**EDHREC Data**: 32% inclusion in optimized lists
**Strategic Impact**: Instant-speed consistency
**Replaces**: Diabolic Intent

#### 14. Force of Will
**Category**: Instant (Counterspell)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Low (instant, one-time)
**Power Level**: 9/10
**Why Add**: Free counterspell that protects combo turns or disrupts opponents when tapped out. At Bracket 4, protecting Muldrotha or stopping opponent wins while holding up no mana is critical. Card disadvantage is mitigated by strong draw engines. Essential interaction for competitive casual.
**EDHREC Data**: Common in high-power lists
**Strategic Impact**: Free interaction, combo protection
**Replaces**: Veil of Summer

#### 15. Birthing Pod
**Category**: Artifact (Tutor Engine)
**Muldrotha Synergy**: Very High (artifact permanent, fills graveyard)
**Power Level**: 8/10 in this deck
**Why Add**: Creature tutor that fills graveyard with both the sacrificed creature and the tutored creature (if Muldrotha isn't online yet). Can be replayed through Muldrotha if destroyed. Creates toolbox lines where you sacrifice 2-drop to get 3-drop, sacrifice that to get 4-drop, building value chains. Enables creature-based answers.
**EDHREC Data**: 12% inclusion but high synergy
**Strategic Impact**: Creature tutor engine
**Replaces**: Necromancy

## Strategic Impact Analysis

### How Changes Support 70/20/10 Strategy

**70% Midrange Value - IMPROVED ✅**

**Removed**:
- Hermit Druid (combo piece)
- Demonic Consultation (combo piece)
- Cards pulling toward combo

**Added**:
- Card advantage engines (Mulldrifter, River Kelpie, Secrets of the Dead)
- Recurrable removal (Seal of Primordium, Haywire Mite)
- Tutors that find value pieces OR combo when needed
- Better ramp for sustained midrange plan

**Result**: Deck now focuses more heavily on grinding value through Muldrotha recursion, with better tools to execute midrange plan.

**Removed**:
- Pitiless Plunderer (non-essential altar piece)

**Kept**:
- All 3 altars (Ashnod's, Phyrexian, Altar of Dementia)
- Gravecrawler, Bloodghast, Reassembling Skeleton
- Syr Konrad (payoff)

**Added**:
- Better tutors to find combo pieces (Demonic Tutor, Vampiric Tutor)
- Birthing Pod (can tutor creatures for combo)

**Result**: Altar package streamlined but more consistent with better tutors.

---

**10% Thoracle Escape Hatch - MAINTAINED ✅**

**Removed**:
- Demonic Consultation (too narrow)

**Kept**:
- Thassa's Oracle
- Entomb (puts Oracle in graveyard if needed)
- All tutors can find Oracle

**Added**:
- Demonic Tutor, Vampiric Tutor (find Oracle OR Consultation alternatives)

**Result**: Thoracle remains emergency win but no longer has dedicated enabler taking deck slot. Oracle can win through natural mill (Hermit Druid replacement, self-mill package) or traditional Consultation if drawn naturally.
